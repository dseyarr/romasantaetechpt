WHAT IS CYBERCRIME?

Cybercrime is any criminal activity that involves a computer, networked device or a network. While most cybercrimes are carried out in order to generate profit for the cybercriminals, some cybercrimes are carried out against computers or devices directly to damage or disable them. Others use computers or networks to spread malware, illegal information, images or other materials. Some cybercrimes do both , target computers to infect them with a computer virus, which is then spread to other machines and, sometimes, entire networks.

THE CYBERCRIMINALS

Cybercriminals may target an individual's private information or corporate data for theft and resale. As many workers settle into remote work routines due to the pandemic, cybercrimes are expected to grow in frequency in 2021, making it especially important to protect backup data.

CYBERCRIME IN THE PHILIPPINES

The Philippine Congress enacted Republic Act No. 10175 or “Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012” which completely address crimes committed against and by means of computer system on 12 September 2012. It includes penal substantive rules, procedural rules and also rules on international cooperation.

In the Philippines, incidences of crime went down to 105,568 from July 2022 to January 7, 2023 against 112,746 in the comparable period in 2021.

P L A T F O R M S

Reduce the risk of cybercrime in our society by encouraging the online population to take proactive steps against it through the sharing of information, as well as by guiding and supporting them as they develop peaceful solutions to the concerns and challenges it raises. Social media has become a powerful tool for sharing information and knowledge. Facebook, Instagram, Youtube, TikTok, and Twitter are the most commonly used social media platforms. ACTIVITIES

Social Media Live

A total of 5.07 billion people around the world use the internet today – equivalent to 63.5 percent of the world's total population. Educating the people about Online Awareness and Cybercrime through Social Media Lives will reach a lot of people. This Social Media Live will talk about how to stay safe online, how to set-up two factor authentications and more needed information about Cybercrime. Also, Social Media Lives allow the viewers to freely ask questions through comment and get the answer right away. Social Media Lives will happen twice a month for two hours.

School Seminar

Nowadays, many students own devices and have social media accounts at a young age. Most of them are still unaware of how these things work —such as emails, passwords, two-factor authentication, and many more. Students are prone to cybercrimes due to their urge to explore online which makes them easy targets. Seminars will open an opportunity for students to be educated regarding this current issue.

Social Media Posts

Posting on Social Media regularly is a helpful way of spreading the message across the globe. The influence of abundance in social media presence will greatly boost dissemination of information and positive influence. Posting content of videos and digital images, that catches the attention of the general population, will make a change and shift minds whether it is big or small.
